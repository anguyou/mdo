Today I went to Subway to pick up a quick lunch—I got a foot-long steak combo with chips and a drink. When I got to the end of the line, I saw the cookies and I thought to myself, "Hell yeah I'm getting cookies!" Having only glanced at the cookie container as I approached, I asked for some chocolate chip cookies.

"We're out, sold them to a bunch of kids right before you," the guy says as he starts to look a bit annoyed.

"Wow, nice—good problem to have, huh?" I say back. As a guy who loves to eat cookies, I'm thinking this is a great problem to have; people want what this guy has and he can't keep up with demand.

"Nope, now I gotta make more." he replies grimly.

"Well yeah, but it's better than having them all and no one buying, right?"

"No, now I have to make more." He pauses and sighs. I back down and just hand him my debit card.

Ignoring the fact that I was at Subway, the moral of the story is this guy—who always looks rundown and irritated when I stop in—is pretty upset that he has to do a bit of work to take more money from customers. It's such a small problem in the grand scheme of things, but it was clearly bringing this guy down.

If you're going to be all pissy about people wanting what you're selling, you're doing it wrong.
